# Pet Emergency Recognition

This document describes signs that warrant immediate veterinary attention. PawPal+ is not a veterinary diagnostic tool — when in doubt, call a vet or pet emergency hotline.

## Critical Emergencies (call vet immediately)

The following symptoms indicate a possible life-threatening emergency. Do not delay scheduling, do not "wait and see":

- Difficulty breathing, gasping, blue or pale gums
- Collapse, sudden inability to stand, or loss of consciousness
- Seizures lasting more than 2 minutes or repeated seizures
- Suspected ingestion of toxic substances (chocolate, xylitol, antifreeze, rat poison, human medications, household cleaners, lilies for cats)
- Bloated, hard, distended abdomen, especially with retching but no vomit (gastric dilation-volvulus / "bloat" — a dog killer if untreated)
- Heavy bleeding that does not stop with 5 minutes of direct pressure
- Severe trauma (hit by car, fall from height, animal attack)
- Inability to urinate, especially in male cats (urinary blockage is fatal within 24-48 hours)
- Heatstroke signs: heavy panting, drooling, vomiting, collapse, body temp over 104°F
- Eye injury, eye protruding from socket
- Suspected broken bone, severe limping with no weight bearing

## Urgent (vet within 12-24 hours)

- Repeated vomiting or diarrhea, especially with blood
- Refusal to eat for more than 24 hours in a dog, more than 12 hours in a cat
- Lethargy combined with other symptoms
- Limping that persists more than 24 hours
- New lumps, bumps, or skin changes
- Excessive water drinking and urination
- Persistent coughing, sneezing, or eye discharge

## Non-Urgent (schedule normal vet appointment)

- Mild stiffness after exercise that resolves with rest
- Single episode of vomiting in an otherwise normal pet
- Mild ear scratching, occasional shaking head
- Routine vaccination schedule
- Annual wellness exams

## Toxic Foods and Substances

Common household items that are toxic to pets:

- **Chocolate** (dogs): dark chocolate is most dangerous, can cause seizures
- **Xylitol** (dogs): in sugar-free gum, peanut butter, baked goods — causes rapid blood sugar crash
- **Grapes and raisins** (dogs): cause kidney failure, even small amounts
- **Onions and garlic** (dogs and cats): cause anemia
- **Lilies** (cats): all parts are deadly, even pollen — call vet immediately
- **Antifreeze**: sweet taste attracts pets, causes fatal kidney failure
- **Human medications**: ibuprofen, acetaminophen, ADHD meds, antidepressants
- **Marijuana / THC**: edibles are especially dangerous
