# Feeding Guidelines for Pets

## Dogs

Adult dogs typically eat two meals per day, spaced about 8 to 12 hours apart. Puppies under six months need three to four smaller meals per day because their stomachs are small and their energy needs are high.

Feeding times should be consistent. A dog that is fed at 7 AM and 6 PM every day will develop a predictable bathroom schedule and is less likely to beg for food at random times.

Skipping a meal is not always an emergency, but a dog that refuses food for more than 24 hours, or a puppy that refuses food for more than 12 hours, should be evaluated by a veterinarian. Loss of appetite combined with lethargy, vomiting, or diarrhea is a red flag that warrants prompt veterinary attention.

Avoid feeding dogs chocolate, grapes, raisins, onions, garlic, xylitol (a sweetener), macadamia nuts, or alcohol. These are toxic and can cause serious harm even in small amounts. If a dog has ingested any of these, contact a veterinarian or poison control immediately.

Fresh water should be available at all times. Refill bowls daily and wash them weekly to prevent bacterial growth.

## Cats

Cats are obligate carnivores and need a diet rich in animal protein. Most adult cats do well with two meals a day, though some prefer to graze on dry food throughout the day.

Wet food is important for hydration, especially because cats often do not drink enough water on their own. Aim for at least one wet meal per day if possible.

A cat that stops eating for more than 24 hours is at serious risk of hepatic lipidosis (fatty liver disease), which can be fatal. This is more urgent than appetite loss in dogs and requires same-day veterinary attention.

Do not feed cats onions, garlic, chocolate, grapes, raisins, raw fish in large quantities, or dog food as a primary diet (it lacks taurine, which cats need).

## Portion Control

Overweight pets have shorter lifespans and higher rates of diabetes, joint disease, and heart problems. Follow the feeding guide on the food package as a starting point, then adjust based on your pet's body condition. You should be able to feel ribs without pressing hard, and there should be a visible waist when viewed from above.

## Scheduling Feeding Tasks

Mark feeding tasks as HIGH priority in PawPal+. Feeding is recurring, typically every 12 hours for adults and every 6 to 8 hours for puppies. The duration is usually short, around 5 to 15 minutes, but consistency matters more than duration.
