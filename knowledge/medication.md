# Medication Administration Guidelines

## General Principles

Medications must be given exactly as prescribed by a veterinarian. Skipping doses, doubling up, or stopping early can cause treatment failure, antibiotic resistance, or serious side effects.

Most oral medications for pets should be given with food unless the prescription specifies otherwise. Food helps reduce stomach upset and improves absorption of fat-soluble medications.

Always read the label for storage instructions. Some medications must be refrigerated, and some lose potency if exposed to light or heat.

## Common Medication Schedules

Antibiotics are typically given twice daily (every 12 hours) for 7 to 14 days. The full course must be completed even if the pet seems better.

Heartworm preventatives are given once monthly, usually on the same date each month. Missing a dose can leave the pet vulnerable.

Flea and tick preventatives may be monthly oral chews or topical liquids. Topical treatments are applied between the shoulder blades where the pet cannot lick them off, and the area should remain dry for 24 hours.

Insulin for diabetic pets is given every 12 hours, typically with meals. Timing and consistency are critical.

Pain medications such as NSAIDs (carprofen, meloxicam) require careful monitoring for kidney and liver function. Do not give human pain medications such as ibuprofen or acetaminophen to pets — they are toxic.

## Missed Doses

If you miss a dose, give it as soon as you remember unless it is almost time for the next dose. Never give a double dose to "catch up." When in doubt, contact the prescribing veterinarian.

For medications given twice daily that are missed by more than 4 hours past the scheduled time, skip that dose and resume on the next scheduled dose.

## Signs of Adverse Reaction

Watch for vomiting, diarrhea, lethargy, loss of appetite, facial swelling, or unusual behavior after starting a new medication. Stop the medication and contact the veterinarian immediately if any of these occur.

## Scheduling Medications in PawPal+

Mark all medication tasks as HIGH priority. Set them as recurring with the appropriate interval (typically 12 hours for twice-daily, 24 hours for once-daily). Use the notes field to record the dose and any special instructions ("with food," "between shoulder blades," etc.). Medication tasks have the highest health-importance weight in the scheduler and will be prioritized over walks and play even at equal priority levels.
