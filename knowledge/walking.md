# Walking and Exercise Guidelines

## Daily Walk Requirements by Breed Size

Small breeds such as Chihuahuas, Pomeranians, and Yorkies typically need 20 to 40 minutes of walking per day, split into two short walks. Their joints and energy systems do not handle long single sessions well.

Medium breeds such as Beagles, Cocker Spaniels, and Shiba Inus need 45 to 60 minutes per day. A morning walk of about 30 minutes plus an evening walk of similar length is a good baseline.

Large and high-energy breeds such as Labradors, Border Collies, German Shepherds, and Huskies need 60 to 120 minutes of vigorous exercise per day. Without enough activity, these breeds often develop destructive behaviors such as chewing furniture, digging, or excessive barking.

Senior dogs (typically 7 years and older for medium and large breeds, 10+ for small breeds) need shorter, gentler walks. Watch for signs of stiffness, limping, or reluctance to continue.

## Weather Considerations

In hot weather (above 80°F or 27°C), walk dogs in the early morning or late evening to avoid paw burns from hot pavement and heat exhaustion. Test the pavement with the back of your hand: if you cannot hold it there for 7 seconds, it is too hot for paws.

In cold weather (below 20°F or -7°C), short-haired and small breeds may need a coat. Watch for shivering, lifting paws, or whining, which indicate the dog is too cold.

Never leave a dog in a parked car. Even at 70°F outside, the interior can reach 100°F in 20 minutes.

## Cats and Exercise

Cats do not typically need walks but do need play sessions. Aim for two play sessions of 10 to 15 minutes per day using wand toys, laser pointers, or puzzle feeders. Indoor cats without enough mental and physical stimulation often become overweight or develop behavioral problems.

## Scheduling Walks in PawPal+

Mark morning walks as HIGH priority and evening walks as MEDIUM priority. The morning walk often serves a bathroom-relief function, which is more critical. Duration should match the breed needs above. If the owner has a tight schedule, prioritize at least one walk of adequate length over two short rushed walks.
