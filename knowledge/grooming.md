# Grooming Guidelines

## Dogs

Grooming frequency depends heavily on coat type. Short-coated breeds such as Beagles and Boxers need brushing once a week and bathing every 4 to 8 weeks. Over-bathing strips natural oils and can cause dry skin.

Double-coated breeds such as Huskies, Shiba Inus, and Golden Retrievers need brushing 2 to 3 times per week, increasing to daily during shedding seasons (spring and fall). They should not be shaved — the double coat insulates against both heat and cold.

Long-coated and curly breeds such as Poodles, Shih Tzus, and Doodles need daily brushing to prevent matting, plus professional grooming every 6 to 8 weeks for trims.

Nail trims are needed every 3 to 4 weeks for most dogs. Overgrown nails can curl into paw pads or cause posture problems. If you can hear nails clicking on hard floors, they are too long.

Ear cleaning is needed monthly for floppy-eared breeds (Cocker Spaniels, Basset Hounds) which trap moisture and develop infections. Use a vet-approved ear cleaner, never water or alcohol, and never insert cotton swabs deep into the ear canal.

Teeth brushing is ideal daily but at minimum 3 times per week. Use pet-specific toothpaste only — human toothpaste contains xylitol which is toxic to dogs.

## Cats

Most cats groom themselves, but assistance varies by coat. Short-haired cats need brushing once a week. Long-haired cats such as Persians and Maine Coons need daily brushing to prevent painful mats.

Cats rarely need baths unless they have gotten into something messy or have a skin condition. Most cats find baths very stressful.

Nail trims are needed every 2 to 4 weeks. Indoor cats need this more often than outdoor cats since they do not naturally wear nails down.

## Anal Glands

Some dogs and cats need their anal glands expressed periodically, typically every 3 to 6 months. Signs that expression is needed include scooting on the floor, licking the area, or a strong fishy odor. This is best done by a vet or groomer.

## Scheduling Grooming in PawPal+

Mark routine brushing as MEDIUM priority and recurring (daily for long coats, every 2 to 3 days for double coats, weekly for short coats). Mark professional grooming appointments as HIGH priority with a fixed time, since they involve external scheduling. Nail trims should be MEDIUM priority and recurring monthly.
