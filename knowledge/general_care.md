# General Daily Care Best Practices

## Building a Routine

Pets thrive on predictability. A consistent daily routine reduces stress, improves house training, and makes behavioral problems easier to spot. The four anchors of a healthy routine are: morning bathroom break and meal, midday activity or check-in, evening meal and exercise, and a calm wind-down before bed.

For working owners who are away during the day, a midday dog walker or a puzzle feeder can help bridge the gap. Cats generally tolerate alone time better than dogs but still benefit from interactive play in the morning and evening.

## Time Budgeting

A typical adult dog requires roughly 2 to 3 hours of direct human interaction per day spread across feeding, walking, training, and play. A typical adult cat requires 30 to 60 minutes of focused interaction. Puppies and kittens need significantly more attention, plus more frequent bathroom breaks and short training sessions.

If your available window is tight, prioritize in this order:
1. Medications (non-negotiable, fixed times)
2. Feeding (twice daily for adults, more for young pets)
3. Bathroom breaks / morning walk
4. Mental stimulation (training, puzzle toys)
5. Extended exercise
6. Grooming maintenance
7. Play

When the schedule is overcommitted, the AI advisor should recommend dropping or shortening lower-priority items first, never medications or feeding.

## Recognizing a Healthy Pet

Daily checks that take less than a minute:
- Bright, clear eyes with no discharge
- Clean ears with no strong smell
- Normal appetite and water intake
- Normal stool and urination
- Normal energy level for the breed and age
- No new lumps, scratches, or hair loss
- Normal gait, no limping

Any deviation from baseline that persists more than 24 hours is worth noting. PawPal+ users can use the notes field on tasks to track observations over time.

## Mental Stimulation

Pets need mental enrichment, not just physical exercise. A 15-minute training session is roughly equivalent to a 30-minute walk in terms of mental tiredness. Puzzle feeders, scent work, new walking routes, and learning new tricks all qualify as enrichment.

Bored pets often develop destructive or anxious behaviors: excessive barking, digging, chewing, over-grooming, or aggression. Adding a daily 10 to 15 minute training or puzzle task to the schedule often resolves these issues.

## Multi-Pet Households

In a multi-pet household, each pet still needs individual attention and individual feeding stations. Cats especially can become stressed if forced to share resources. Aim for one litter box per cat plus one extra, and feed dogs in separate locations if there is any sign of resource guarding.

The PawPal+ scheduler should distribute attention across pets within the available window, not concentrate all tasks on one pet.

## Owner Self-Care

Pet care is sustainable only if the owner is also resourced. Burned-out owners skip walks, miss medications, and develop resentment toward their pets. Build in flexibility: it is fine to swap a long walk for a short walk when the owner is exhausted, as long as the core needs (food, water, bathroom, medication) are met.
